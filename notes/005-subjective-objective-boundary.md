# Subjective, Objective, and Boundary Before Body

Status: working theory note
Date: 2026-06-01
Scope: TAT framing for subjectivity, objectivity, boundary, and bodyhood

## 1. Purpose

This note records a Thalean Action Theory position on two linked questions:

1. What does TAT say about the subjective and the objective?
2. What does TAT mean by boundary before body?

The central answer is:

Subjectivity is frame-local witness.
Objectivity is frame-crossing invariance.
Bodyhood begins when a boundary makes relational persistence possible.

This note should be read as a conceptual bridge between existential relativity, discrete action theory, and the logic stack.

## 2. The subjective/objective distinction

In TAT, the subjective and objective are not treated as enemies.

The subjective is not simply fake, private, irrational, or useless.

The objective is not a view from nowhere.

Instead:

The subjective is what becomes legible from inside a particular relational frame.

The objective is what remains invariant across a disciplined family of relational frames.

Short form:

Subjective truth is frame-local legibility.
Objective truth is frame-crossing invariance.

This makes the subjective a real witness, but not a completed court case.

It makes the objective a disciplined achievement, not a magical escape from perspective.

## 3. Subjectivity as local witness

A subject is a relational position with orientation, access limits, history, memory, attention, vulnerability, and boundary conditions.

A subject sees from somewhere.

That somewhere is not an error. It is part of the data.

TAT therefore treats subjective experience as situated witness.

A subjective report says:

From this position, under these boundaries, with this history and access, this is what became legible.

That is meaningful.

But it is not automatically global.

The statement:

I experienced X.

is not the same kind of claim as:

X is globally invariant across all relevant frames.

The first is a frame-local witness claim.
The second is an objectivity claim.

TAT preserves this distinction without humiliating the subjective.

## 4. Objectivity as disciplined translation

Objectivity is not the absence of perspective.

There is no outside-all-relation position.

Instead, objectivity is what happens when a claim, structure, or pattern can be carried across frames without losing its defining structure.

Objectivity is disciplined translation.

A claim becomes more objective when it survives:

changed viewpoint,
changed observer,
changed boundary,
changed scale,
changed notation,
changed projection,
formalization,
measurement,
adversarial testing,
and loss of decorative context.

Objectivity is therefore multi-frame accountability.

In TAT language:

Objectivity is stable transport across relational frames.

## 5. Existential relativity and objectivity

Existential relativity says that identity is not an isolated property of a thing, but a relational invariant revealed across frames.

This does not destroy objectivity. It gives objectivity a stricter job.

Objectivity is not what remains when relation is removed.

Objectivity is what survives when relation is varied.

This is a major distinction.

A naive objectivism may want truth without frame.

TAT asks for truth that survives frame-change.

The question is not:

Can I escape all perspectives?

The question is:

What remains accountable across disciplined changes of perspective?

## 6. Boundary before body

Boundary before body is a foundational TAT phrase.

A body does not appear first as pure substance.

A body becomes legible when a boundary distinguishes inside from outside, self from environment, object from field, actor from context.

Without a boundary, there is no stable thing to discuss.

There is only undifferentiated field, flux, or relation.

Boundary is therefore the first relational act by which a body becomes identifiable.

Short form:

Boundary is the first condition of body.

## 7. What a boundary does

A boundary is not merely a wall.

A boundary is an interface.

It decides:

what counts as inside,
what counts as outside,
what may enter,
what may leave,
what must be translated,
what is preserved,
what is rejected,
what counts as signal,
what counts as noise,
what actions are admissible,
and what exchanges are allowed.

In TAT language:

A boundary is an admissibility surface.

This is one of the strongest formulations in this note.

A boundary is the surface where relation is regulated.

It does not merely separate. It governs exchange.

## 8. Body as bounded relational persistence

A body is not merely matter occupying space.

A body is a bounded relational persistence.

Working definition:

A body is a bounded relational object whose identity is preserved across a family of admissible transformations.

This applies across domains.

A physical organism persists through metabolism.

A graph object persists through relabeling and invariant-preserving transformations.

A person persists through memory, relation, role, and change.

A theory persists through critique, translation, and formal pressure.

A thalion persists through its full relational structure, not merely through a quotient-visible shadow.

Bodyhood is therefore not only about having an inside. It is about preserving identity across regulated exchange with an outside.

## 9. Boundary and subjectivity

The subjective depends on boundary.

To have a subjective perspective is to have an inside from which the world is encountered.

The boundary may be biological, cognitive, social, linguistic, instrumental, formal, or methodological.

A subjective report is generated from within a boundary.

So TAT says:

The subjective is the view from within a boundary.

This does not make it false.

It makes it situated.

To evaluate it, we ask:

What boundary produced this witness?
What did the witness have access to?
What was excluded?
What could cross the boundary?
What could not?
What transformations preserve the report?
What changes when the boundary changes?

## 10. Boundary and objectivity

Objectivity depends on boundary change.

A claim becomes more objective when it survives disciplined boundary variation.

If the boundary is "my felt experience," then a claim may be subjectively valid.

If the boundary is "multiple observers under repeatable conditions," then the claim must survive a larger frame.

If the boundary is "formal graph invariant," then the claim must survive relabeling and isomorphism.

If the boundary is "physical model," then the claim must survive measurement, prediction, and empirical constraint.

Therefore TAT does not first ask:

Is this subjective or objective?

It asks:

Relative to which boundary, frame, and admissible transformations is this claim being made?

This is the sharper question.

## 11. Boundary as admissibility surface

The phrase "admissibility surface" connects boundary to discrete action theory.

Discrete action theory studies lawful finite transformations inside relational structures.

A boundary helps determine which transformations are lawful.

It may allow, forbid, translate, buffer, filter, or transform actions crossing from one side to another.

In this sense, boundary is not passive.

Boundary participates in action.

A boundary defines an action interface.

It tells the system which exchanges count as admissible and which destroy the identity of the body.

## 12. Boundary, body, and field effect

A body receives constraint through its boundary.

A body also emits constraint through its boundary.

This is where field effect enters.

A body is not merely shaped by its environment. It changes the relational field around itself.

Its presence may change:

paths,
distances,
cycles,
symmetries,
bottlenecks,
signals,
available actions,
interpretive frames,
and global coherence.

This gives the active TAT formulation:

A thing is what its relations make possible, and what its presence makes possible for others.

A boundary is where this double movement becomes visible.

Constraint enters.
Constraint exits.
Identity is preserved or lost through the exchange.

## 13. Subjective witness and objective witness

Within TAT, it is useful to distinguish subjective witness and objective witness.

A subjective witness is a trace from within a boundary.

An objective witness is a trace that survives disciplined transport across boundaries or frames.

Neither is trivial.

The subjective witness gives local access.

The objective witness gives cross-frame accountability.

In the current graph-theory/G900 witness language, this distinction should be handled carefully. "Subjective witness" and "objective witness" may also refer to specific named graph witnesses in a technical context. This note uses the terms philosophically unless otherwise specified.

## 14. Relation to coherence gap

A coherence gap often occurs when a surface is convincing inside one boundary but fails across a wider frame.

A claim may be locally legible but globally incoherent.

Subjective surfaces can be locally real without being globally stable.

Objective structure requires survival across frame-change.

In TAT language:

A coherence gap is a failure of frame-local appearance to become frame-crossing invariant.

This does not mean the local appearance was meaningless.

It means the local appearance was not yet accountable as global structure.

## 15. Relation to CoRI

CoRI turns this into method.

CoRI asks how apparent patterns become accountable structures by testing whether they survive changed viewpoints, controlled perturbations, and traceable constraints.

In the language of this note:

CoRI tests whether subjective or local witness can become objective or cross-frame witness.

It asks:

What boundary produced the appearance?
What happens when the boundary changes?
What remains invariant?
What fails?
What was decorative context?
What was constraint-bearing context?

This is how local surface becomes accountable structure.

## 16. Public-facing formulations

Short:

Subjectivity is frame-local witness. Objectivity is frame-crossing invariance. Boundary is the first condition of body.

Medium:

TAT does not treat the subjective as fake or the objective as view-from-nowhere. A subjective claim is a witness from inside a boundary. An objective claim is one that survives disciplined movement across boundaries and frames. Bodyhood begins when a boundary makes relational persistence possible.

Strong:

A boundary is an admissibility surface. It is where inside and outside become distinguishable, where exchange becomes regulated, and where a body first becomes testable as a persistent relational object.

## 17. Slogan set

The subjective is the view from within a boundary.

The objective is what survives across boundary changes.

Boundary is the first condition of body.

A boundary is not merely a wall. It is an admissibility surface.

A body is a bounded relation that persists.

Objectivity is not the absence of perspective. It is the survival of structure across perspectives.

Subjectivity is not error. It is local witness.

Boundary is where relation becomes regulated exchange.

A body is what remains coherent through boundary-preserving transformation.

## 18. Summary

TAT reframes the subjective/objective divide as a question of frame and invariant.

The subjective is frame-local witness.

The objective is frame-crossing invariance.

Boundary comes before body because a body becomes legible only when a boundary distinguishes inside from outside and regulates admissible exchange.

A body is therefore not isolated substance. It is bounded relational persistence.
