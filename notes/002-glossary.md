# Thalean Relational Theory Glossary

Status: working glossary
Date: 2026-06-01
Scope: stable vocabulary for the relational-theory repo

## Purpose

This glossary collects the core terms used in Thalean Relational Theory.

The goal is not to freeze every definition permanently. The goal is to make the current working vocabulary explicit enough that future notes, papers, code, and public explanations can use terms consistently.

## Thalean Relational Theory

Thalean Relational Theory, or TRT, is the umbrella research program studying how identity, meaning, action, and accountability arise through relational structure, admissible transformation, and invariants that survive changed frames.

Core maxim:

Identity is not isolated substance. Identity is relational invariance under admissible change.

Operational maxim:

A thing is what its relations make possible, and what its presence makes possible for others.

## Thalean Graph Theory

Thalean Graph Theory, or TGT, is the mathematical graph-theoretic core inside Thalean Relational Theory.

TGT studies finite relational structures whose vertices, edges, sectors, quotients, incidence laws, transport patterns, and invariants can be made explicit and tested.

In the current project, TGT includes the Thalean graph, thalions, sector systems, quotient witnesses, incidence matrices, quadratic shadows, transport structures, and related finite graph constructions.

## Thalean

Thalean names the project lineage and scope.

The term points to Thales as a historical symbol of the movement from inherited image toward accountable structure. In this project, the Thalean move is to receive intuition, symbol, or appearance, then ask what relational structure, admissible action, or invariant test can make it accountable.

The term is not intended as mythological decoration. It is a scope marker for the body of work.

## Thalean graph

A Thalean graph is a graph-level relational object in the Thalean Graph Theory program.

The current central Thalean graph is the 60-vertex organism associated with AT4val[60,6], also known in related graph catalog contexts as Graph52002. It is treated as a full graph-level structure, not merely as a drawing, quotient, or projection.

## Thalion

A thalion is a single concrete functional cluster-instance of the Thalean graph organism.

In the current locked terminology, a thalion refers specifically to the G60 form: the concrete 60-state transport organism or instance with its full relationships and properties.

The G15 sector system, matrix M, quadratic Q, and cubic law are downstream quotient-visible shadows or compressed legibility layers of the thalion, not the thalion itself.

## Relation

A relation is a structured connection, constraint, incidence, accessibility, adjacency, dependency, or transformation rule between elements.

In TRT, relations are not merely decorative links between already complete things. Relations may help constitute what a thing is, what it can do, how it can be interpreted, and how it changes the surrounding field.

## Relational frame

A relational frame is the context in which entities, relations, labels, and admissible actions are evaluated.

A basic graph can serve as a relational frame. A modal frame can also serve as a relational frame. More generally, a relational frame is any structured setting in which identity and action become legible.

A relational frame may include:

vertices or states,
relations or edges,
labels,
typed accessibility relations,
admissibility rules,
actions,
and invariants.

## Identity

Identity is the recognizable structure of a thing across relevant frames and transformations.

In TRT, identity is not treated as isolated substance first. Identity is treated as a relational invariant under admissible change.

This means an entity is understood by:

the relations that make it legible,
the actions it admits,
the transformations it survives,
and the changes it induces around itself.

## Existential relativity

Existential relativity is the philosophical thesis inside TRT.

Working definition:

Existential relativity is the view that identity is not an isolated property of a thing, but a relational invariant revealed across frames.

The analogy to general relativity is limited and philosophical. General relativity concerns physical measurement and frame dependence. Existential relativity concerns meaning, identity, relation, and accountable structure.

Existential relativity is not lazy relativism. It does not say anything can mean anything. It says that whatever is true or structurally accountable must survive the work of relation.

## Discrete Action Theory

Discrete Action Theory is the operational dynamics inside TRT.

It studies lawful finite transformation inside relational structures.

An action has:

a before-state,
an after-state,
a local context,
a rule of admissibility,
a trace,
and an effect on the surrounding possibility-space.

Discrete Action Theory asks which actions are admissible, which preserve identity, which expose hidden structure, which collapse a surface, and which alter the relational field around them.

## Action

An action is a transition or transformation inside a relational frame.

An action is not merely motion. It is a structured change whose meaning depends on the frame, its admissibility rule, and its effects.

In graph language, an action may be a move, traversal, update, activation, projection, lift, quotient, perturbation, or other lawful transformation.

## Admissibility

Admissibility is the rule or condition that determines whether an action is lawful within a given relational frame.

Not every imaginable transformation is relevant to identity. TRT focuses on admissible transformations because those are the lawful tests of structure.

Admissibility separates arbitrary mutation from meaningful action.

## Invariant

An invariant is a property, pattern, relation, or profile that remains stable under a specified family of transformations.

In TRT, invariants are central because they distinguish accountable structure from local surface appearance.

A proposed identity becomes stronger when its defining features survive changed viewpoint, perturbation, relabeling, projection, or admissible transformation.

## Existential invariant

An existential invariant is a property or relational profile of an entity that is preserved across a specified family of admissible frame transformations.

Plain version:

An existential invariant is what remains recognizably true of a thing after the relevant lawful changes have been applied.

## Field effect

Field effect is the change an entity or action induces in the relational frame around it.

A thing is not only shaped by its relations. It may reshape the possibility-space around itself.

In graph language, a field effect may change paths, distances, cycles, symmetries, bottlenecks, admissible actions, local neighborhoods, or global coherence.

Core formulation:

A thing is what its relations make possible, and what its presence makes possible for others.

## Surface

A surface is the locally visible or projected appearance of a structure.

A surface may be faithful, misleading, incomplete, or locally convincing but globally incoherent.

In CoRI and coherence-gap work, a surface must be tested against deeper relational structure before being trusted.

## Witness

A witness is a concrete object, artifact, calculation, graph, table, program output, visualization, or proof element that makes a claim inspectable.

A witness does not merely assert that something is true. It gives the reader or investigator something to check.

In TRT, witnesses are part of accountability.

## Coherence gap

A coherence gap is a mismatch between local appearance and global structure.

A system may produce a locally convincing surface while failing to preserve deeper relational coherence.

The coherence-gap method tests whether apparent patterns survive changed viewpoints, perturbations, and traceable constraints.

## Projection

A projection is a compressed or lower-dimensional view of a larger structure.

A projection may reveal useful structure, but it can also hide context, collapse distinctions, or create misleading local surfaces.

In the Thalean stack, quotient-visible objects such as G15 structures are projections or compressed legibility layers of the fuller graph-level organism.

## Quotient

A quotient is a structure formed by identifying, grouping, or compressing parts of a larger structure according to a rule.

Quotients are useful because they can reveal patterns that are difficult to see in the full object. They are dangerous when mistaken for the whole object.

## Shadow

A shadow is a downstream, compressed, quotient-visible, or projected trace of a fuller structure.

A shadow may be mathematically important and highly informative, but it should not automatically be treated as the full organism.

## Dynamic relational modal logic

Dynamic relational modal logic is the candidate formal language for TRT.

The phrase names a stack rather than a single finished calculus:

relational graph structure,
typed modal logic,
temporal logic,
dynamic logic,
admissibility logic,
and update or graph-transformation semantics.

The core need is a language that can express:

what is possible from here,
what is necessary across accessible alternatives,
which actions are admissible,
what those actions preserve,
and how those actions change what becomes possible next.

## Modal frame

A modal frame is a set of worlds or states together with an accessibility relation.

In TRT terms, a modal frame is graph-like. It gives a way to ask what is possible, necessary, reachable, or preserved from a given position.

## Accessibility relation

An accessibility relation says which states, worlds, positions, or frames are reachable from which others.

In graph language, accessibility is edge-like. In modal logic, accessibility determines the meaning of possibility and necessity.

## Typed relation

A typed relation is a relation whose kind matters.

Not all relations should be collapsed into a generic edge. Adjacency, incidence, transport, memory, observation, projection, and admissible action may each need distinct relation types.

Typed relations allow TRT to distinguish different kinds of relational dependence.

## Update semantics

Update semantics studies how actions change not only propositions inside a frame, but the frame itself.

This matters for TRT because field effect requires more than movement inside a fixed structure. Actions may change the accessibility relations, labels, admissibility rules, or available states around them.

## CoRI

CoRI, the Center of Recursive Inquiry, is the research method and institutional wrapper associated with this work.

Its public thesis:

CoRI studies how apparent patterns become accountable structures by testing whether they survive changed viewpoints, controlled perturbations, and traceable constraints.

In TRT terms, CoRI supplies the discipline of witness, audit, and coherence testing.

## Accountability

Accountability means that a claim, structure, or pattern can be traced, tested, checked, and constrained.

A pattern becomes accountable when it survives more than one favorable description. It should remain legible under changed viewpoint, perturbation, formalization, or independent witness.

## Local mimicry

Local mimicry occurs when a surface looks convincing in local features while failing deeper coherence tests.

The term is useful for AI-generated text, graph projections, philosophical arguments, and scientific models that imitate the appearance of structure without preserving the structure itself.

## Global coherence

Global coherence is the preservation of structure across the wider relational system.

A locally convincing surface may fail global coherence if it contradicts distant constraints, breaks under relabeling, fails perturbation tests, or cannot be integrated into the full frame.

## Constraint-bearing context

Constraint-bearing context is context that changes what interpretations, actions, or transformations are admissible.

It differs from decorative context, which may improve readability or presentation but does not materially constrain the structure.

In TRT, constraint-bearing context is central because identity depends on relations that actually restrict or guide possibility.

## Decorative context

Decorative context adds style, readability, naming, metaphor, or presentation without materially constraining the underlying structure.

Decorative context is not bad. It becomes dangerous only when mistaken for constraint-bearing structure.

## Current working slogan set

Identity is not isolated substance. Identity is relational invariance under admissible change.

A thing is what its relations make possible, and what its presence makes possible for others.

To exist is to occupy a relational position that both receives constraint and emits constraint.

Do not protect the idea. Make the idea protect itself.

The question is not whether truth disappears into perspective. The question is what survives the work of relation.
